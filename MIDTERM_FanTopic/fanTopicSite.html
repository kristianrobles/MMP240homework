Introduction to F1 Racing.

In the automotive world, F1 is the most highest sanctioned racing division. Owned by the Formula One Group that was started in its first inagural season in 1950. As rules and technology continue to progress and advance, F1 racing cars can top up to speeds of 230 mph and cornering forces of 8g's. Maintaining and to make an F1 team operational can also cost around $120 million minimum. This equates to one of the most exciting and exhilarating forms of motorsport racing to watch with millions of fans around the globe.
